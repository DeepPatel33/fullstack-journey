Rich Dad Poor Dad - Robert Kiyosaki and Sharon Lechter

Rich Dad Poor Dad: What the Rich Teach Their Kids About Money That the Poor and Middle Class Do Not! is a 1997 book written by Robert T. Kiyosaki and Sharon Lechter.

Summary

Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert T. Kiyosaki contrasts two father figures: Kiyosaki's biological father ("Poor Dad," an educated but financially struggling government employee) and his best friend's father ("Rich Dad," a high school dropout who built wealth through entrepreneurship and investing). The core lesson is to prioritize financial literacy, understanding that the rich have money work for them by acquiring assets (cash-generating) rather than liabilities (cash-draining), breaking free from the "rat race" of working just for a paycheck, and focusing on building your own business and investments.

Why You Reccomend it

ich Dad Poor Dad is widely recommended for its powerful mindset shift on money, teaching you to build assets, make money work for you (not the other way around), and achieve financial independence, contrasting traditional advice with entrepreneurial thinking through relatable stories of two father figures. It's a great starting point for financial literacy, emphasizing taking control, learning financial skills, and understanding assets vs. liabilities to create wealth and escape the "rat race".
